About Dancers' Symposium

Dancers' Symposium (DS) aims to provide an open and supportive environment to further the exposure of dance, music, student choreography, and student performance at Carnegie Mellon University. DS is CMU's largest student-run dance organization, with over 100 members each semester.

History

DS was founded in 1990 by a group of faculty and students to provide an opportunity for graduate and undergraduate students to choreograph and perform original dance pieces for their peers, faculty, and family.

Dancers

Over 130 dancers participate in our annual show each semester, performing in styles from contemporary to tap to hip hop. Our members range from studio-trained dancers who rocked the competition circuit to people who had never danced before DS.

All are welcome to audition, no matter what your skill level--we love seeing new talent! For more information on how to join DS and the audition process, click here. For even more information, feel free to contact us!

Choreographers

Each semester, about 20 dancers choose to choreograph their own pieces, and are given full authority--from choosing to dancers to choosing costumes to choosing songs. Find out how to become a choreographer for our fall 2015 show!

DS: Rewind (Spring '15) Choreographers included: So-Hee Woo, Madeline Hyde, Lisa Natale, Amy Lee, Sam Ahmed, Eric Wong, Job Bedford, Carlos Domene, Jackie Lee, Mindy Pile, Karin Tsai, Emily Mooney, Kaalen Kirrene, Kelsey Scott, Terence An, Katie Bradford, Jaimee Fair, Michelle Cho, David Buzzell, Karon Hawkins, Angelina Sung, Sabrina Liu, and James Harvey.

The directors are a group of 4-6 members of DS responsible for allocating funds, scheduling space, and organizing the show and other events. Directors are chosen at the end of eligible semesters.
